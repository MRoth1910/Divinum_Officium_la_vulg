\mycentering{parish.}

The Church of the Assumption is a parish of the diocese of Nashville founded in 1858. 
After the church was devastated by a tornado in March 2020, Rev.\@ Fr.\@ S.\@ Bede Price, the pastor, oversaw a major restoration executed artistically by Conrad Schmitt Studios, based on photos of the late nineteenth century interior. The restored beauty manifests the church's worthiness
for offering divine worship, making this temple suitable for becoming a center of sacred music in conformity to church teaching and tradition,
with Gregorian chant always at the heart of the parish's spiritual diet, a necessity for witnessing to the Gospel of Our Lord and Redeemer, whose heart speaks unto our own hearts.

\mycentering{music.}
The men's schola, an all-volunteer group seeking to worship God as he desires, sings the chant for Sunday and feast-day Masses, Vespers, and other services, augmented by the ladies of the parish. These volunteers desire to make more widely known the church's treasury of sacred music, ornamenting Vespers of feasts and high Masses during the week by participating in a new volunteer choir dedicated to singing sacred polyphonic and choral music.

We depend on your generosity. Please consider donating to the men's schola fund at 
\url{https://pushpay.com/g/assumptionnashville}, in selecting \emph{Men's schola} from the menu. Donations are tax-deductible. ``Let us bless the Lord. Thanks be to God.''
