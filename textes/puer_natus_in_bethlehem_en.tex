\begin{enpars}

A child is born in Bethlehem; wherefore Jerusalem rejoices. ℟. In jubilation of heart, let us adore the newborn Christ with a new song.

The Son hath assumed flesh, the Most High of God the Father. ℟. In jubilation.

Through the messenger Gabriel, the Virgin conceived a Son. ℟. In jubilation.

Like a bridegroom from his chamber, he came forth from his Mother’s womb. ℟. In jubilation.

He lies in the manger, who reigns without end. ℟. In jubilation.

And the Angel reveals to the shepherds that this is the Lord. ℟. In jubilation.

Kings from Saba come, offering gold, incense, and myrrh. ℟. In jubilation.

Entering the house, in turn they greet the newborn Prince. ℟. In jubilation.

Of a Virgin Mother born, [Him] Who is Light of Light. ℟. In jubilation.

Without the serpent’s wound, he came of our blood. ℟. In jubilation.

Like unto us in flesh, yet unlike us as to sin. ℟. In jubilation.

In order to render us men like unto God and himself. ℟. In jubilation.

On this joyous natal day, let us bless the Lord. ℟. In jubilation.
\end{enpars}
\end{multicols}
