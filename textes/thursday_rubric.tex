\rubrique{Today and the following two days,\normaltext{Pater noster, Ave María,} and \normaltext{Credo.} are said silently. Matins begins absolutely from the antiphon of the first psalm. At the end of each psalm of Matins and of Lauds, one of the fifteen candles of the triangular candelabra placed before the altar.}

\rubrique{At all of the Hours until None of Holy Saturday inclusive, the \normaltext{Glória Patri} is omitted at the end of the psalms and in the responsories. In the sung office, the last verse of the psalms and canticles ends as do the others, namely on the final of the respective antiphon.}

\rubrique{In the merely recited office, at all hours, the voice is lowered by a whole step at the end of the psalms, canticles, and the divisions of these. This is also the case at the end of the psalm \normaltext{Miserére} which is said after the Hours in a lower voice.}

\rubrique{The prayer \normaltext{Réspice} is said on one note in a lower tone, and the last syllable is lowered as in the psalms.}
